EXECUTIVE COMMITTE Oﬃcers shown above plus Peter Butterﬁeld, Jonathan Hyland & Andrew Rawnsley

  1. The Association:
    1. The Association shall be named “The Halifax Cricket League Association of Cricket Oﬃcials” (HXACO).
    2. The Association shall comprise all oﬃcials on the annual list of the Halifax Cricket League (HCL).
    3. All members appointed to the oﬃcial list must be members of the ECB/ACO.
  2. The Objectives of the Association are :
    1. To create and maintain a spirit of comradeship, to improve the eﬃciency of all oﬃcials and to secure uniformity of action on the ﬁeld of play.
    2. To co-operate with the HCL Council in maintaining sportsmanship and good conduct within the HCL and to assist the HCL Executive to further the progress of the League
  3. The Association shall be governed by an Executive Committee (Executive) which shall comprise all the elected Oﬃcers plus up to six additional members. All Oﬃcers shall be elected by the membership of the Association, usually at the Annual General Meeting (AGM), which shall be held before the end of October. Any vacancies to an elected oﬃce occurring between AGMs shall be ratiﬁed at the ﬁrst available meeting of the Association.
  4. The annual subscription shall be £10.00, plus the members annual subscription to the ECB/ACO. The fee to the Association shall be paid to the Treasurer by no later than 31 May. Failure to pay by the due date shall incur a ﬁne of £10.00, thus increasing the subscription fee to £20.00. The Executive shall also have the discretion to withdraw appointments.
  5. The Association shall meet each month during the cricket season. Dates shall be determined at the AGM.
  6. Any oﬃcials accepting an appointment shall adhere to the Rules shown in the HCL Yearbook. Failure to observe may result in the withdrawal of appointments.
  7. All new oﬃcials shall take a test to the satisfaction of the Association Executive before receiving appointments.
  8. Should any question arise in connection with this Association that is not dealt with in these Rules, the Executive shall have full power to deal with it and inﬂict such penalties as they deem expedient and its decision shall be ﬁnal.
  1. Umpires for all League and Cup matches shall be selected from members of the Association.
  2. The umpires’ consolidated expenses shall be in accordance with HCL Rule L11(d).
  3. If no play takes place at any match because of inclement weather the umpires’ consolidated expenses shall also be as (b) above.
  4. In the case of Cup and Shield ties which take longer than one day to complete, and a substitute umpire or umpires have to be used, they shall contact one another at the completion of the tie and share the tie and additional expenses proportionally as speciﬁed in HCL Rule K8 Umpires.
  5. Umpires shall be on the ground at least 30 minutes before the scheduled starting time of a match, and shall report to the home captain
  6. Umpires shall stop cricketing practice ﬁve minutes before the scheduled starting time of the match.
  7. In order to facilitate play at the earliest possible moment, where inclement weather intervenes, the umpires shall ensure that the foot holes made by batsmen and bowlers are dried, cleaned out, and ﬁlled with an appropriate material at any time during the match, including when the match is not actually in progress. They shall report any home club that fails to make every eﬀort to ensure the ground is made ﬁt for play before and during the match. All late starts other than for inclement weather shall be reported with the cause for the delay. An average of 18 overs per hour shall be maintained throughout the match and must be reported if it is not attained. Allowance shall be made during the ﬁnal stages of an innings where the usual increase in runs takes place and more wickets are likely to fall.
  8. Umpires shall not allow practice deliveries during an innings.
  9. Umpires shall wait until any signal has been answered by the scorer before allowing the match to proceed.
  10. Umpires must report any ungentlemanly conduct on the part of the players or spectators on the ﬁeld of play. They must inform the captain of the nature of such reports before the end of the match. They shall also report any team which has less than ten players at the end of the ﬁrst innings. All reports should be signed by both umpires.
  11. It is a requirement that all umpires oﬃciating at matches shall wear white footwear, black or navy trousers, white shirt, a tie and a white coat, the latter two being optional in above average temperatures.
  12. The Roy Smith Club Sportsmanship Cards: one card should be sent to the Appointments Secretary for every League match. In First XI matches the umpires shall decide who sends it in, but both should agree on the marks awarded. In Second XI matches, the neutral umpire shall send it in and he may ask the Club umpire for his/her opinion. Marks should be awarded on the basis of the play and general attitude of both clubs.
  1. Boundaries - a 4 shall be signalled by waving of the arm from side to side and a 6 should be signalled by raising both arms upright above the head.
  2. Byes shall be signalled by raising the open hand above the head.
  3. Leg Byes shall be signalled by raising the leg and touching it with one hand.
  4. Wides shall be signalled by extending both arms horizontally.
  5. No balls shall be signalled by extending one arm horizontally
  6. The decision “Out” shall be signalled by raising the index ﬁnger above the head.
  7. Besides signalling, the umpire shall “call” distinctly for the information of the players.
  8. On giving a decision, the umpires should make sure that the batsman understands perfectly what the decision is.

Any umpire ﬁnding themselves unable to keep an appointment must inform the Appointments Secretary at once. He/she shall appoint a substitute. In such cases prompt action is necessary to allow time for other appointments to be made.
